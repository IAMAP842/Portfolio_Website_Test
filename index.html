    COMMENT TOXICITY LEVEL DETECTION

    I developed a model to classify toxic comments using the Jigsaw Toxic Comment Classification Challenge dataset, categorizing comments into six types: toxic, severe toxic, obscene, threat, insult, and identity hate. The text data was vectorized using TextVectorization and split into training (70%), validation (20%), and test sets (10%). To optimize training efficiency, techniques such as caching, shuffling, batching, and prefetching were employed. The model was trained using Binary Crossentropy loss and the Adam optimizer for 1 epoch. After training, the model was saved, and an interactive Gradio interface was created for real-time classification of toxic comments.

    SPAM MAIL PREDICTION SYSTEM

    The system preprocesses mail data to extract TF-IDF features, enabling efficient prediction of spam or ham mails. Through the implementation of logistic regression alongside TF-IDF, it achieved impressive accuracy rates of 96.7% during training and 96.6% on the test dataset. This approach ensures robust identification of spam mails based on their unique textual characteristics, demonstrating its effectiveness in classifying messages accurately and reliably.
